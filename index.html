Alpha-Helical Regions

Beta-Sheet Network

Structural Biology

From sequence
to function

A protein's one-dimensional amino acid sequence encodes every level of its architecture — from local secondary motifs all the way to multi-subunit quaternary assemblies. Understanding that hierarchy is the key to rational drug design, synthetic biology, and disease research.

I

Primary Structure

The linear sequence of amino acids encoded by the gene — the blueprint from which all higher-order structure emerges.

II

Secondary Structure

Local hydrogen-bonding patterns fold segments into alpha helices, beta sheets, and turns — the recurring vocabulary of protein architecture.

III

Tertiary Structure

The complete three-dimensional fold — the unique, functional shape that evolution has refined over billions of years of selection.
